About the 17 SDGs

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a global initiative adopted by the United Nations in 2015 with the aim of achieving peace, prosperity, and environmental sustainability by the year 2030. These goals were created to address major global challenges such as poverty, hunger, health issues, lack of education, gender inequality, environmental degradation, and climate change.

There are 17 interconnected goals, each focusing on a specific area of sustainable development. Together, they promote balanced growth by ensuring economic progress, social inclusion, and environmental protection. The SDGs encourage countries to work collectively so that development today does not harm future generations.

The 17 SDGs include goals like ending poverty and hunger, ensuring good health and quality education, providing clean water and affordable energy, promoting decent work and innovation, building sustainable cities, protecting life on land and below water, and taking urgent action against climate change.

This project presents the Sustainable Development Goals in an interactive and visual manner. By using maps, charts, and data visualization, it helps users understand how different countries, including India, are progressing towards achieving the SDGs. The aim is to spread awareness and encourage responsible actions that contribute to a sustainable and better future for all.
